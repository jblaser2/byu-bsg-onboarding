# FAQ ❓

The questions every new member asks — answered honestly (and only slightly satirically).

## "I have no idea what I'm supposed to be doing. Is that normal?"

**Extremely.** It's so normal that this entire website exists because of it. Read
[About Me](about-me.md) — I had a headache for three months. The "hands-off" culture means nobody
hands you a task list; your job is to ask questions and find ways to contribute. It feels
uncomfortable, then it becomes the best part.

**What to do right now:** set up a recurring meeting with Dr. Hart or a teammate (see
[Who's Who](contacts.md)), and start working through [Getting Started](getting-started.md).

## "Do I really have to work 10 hours a week?"

Yes — 10 hours is the standard baseline. But "work" is broad: reading about the field, learning a
new programming language, meeting with researchers, problem-solving, and writing up your work all
count. What your days actually look like is up to you. See
[the 10-hour standard](index.md#group-culture-startup-not-assembly-line).

## "How do I get on the supercomputer?"

Go to [rc.byu.edu](https://rc.byu.edu/), request an account, and — seriously — **visit the Research
Computing office in person** for their intro course. Full walkthrough on
[Getting Started](getting-started.md).

## "I don't know what half these words mean."

That's what the [Glossary](glossary.md) is for. CryoET, STA, T4SS, tomogram, PI — all decoded there.

## "Do I need to be a machine-learning or CryoEM expert before I'm useful?"

No. Build enough intuition to follow conversations and read the code, then learn the rest *on* a
real problem. Start with [Understanding CryoEM](cryoem.md) and [Machine Learning](machine-learning.md)
— skim first, master later.

## "What's Tea & Chat? Do I have to go?"

It's Dr. Hart's weekly meeting where the group drinks homemade tea and talks about life, cool
things we've learned, a shared book, and hard research problems. You should go — it's where you'll
actually get to know the group, and where a lot of good thinking happens. 🍵

## "Wait, there's handball?"

There is. 🏐 Dr. Hart is president of the handball club and loves when students play. Beat him and
you might win **\$100.** (No, we can't confirm the current win rate. It's low.)

## "Do I have to publish a paper?"

You should aim to. Writing and submitting at least one article for publication while you're here is
a goal worth setting — it's how the field shares and builds on work, and it's great for you.

## "Should I go to conferences?"

Yes, whenever you get the chance. They're for finding collaborators, learning the state of the art,
and getting feedback. See [Conferences](conferences.md) — and consider building a personal website
to bring along.

## "I'm reading and my brain hurts."

Good — that's learning. But protect the priorities: relationships, health, then learning. Rest is
part of the work. Now go make some tea.

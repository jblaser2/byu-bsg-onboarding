# Glossary 📖

The field is drowning in acronyms and jargon. Nobody's born knowing this stuff — here's the decoder
ring. (If a term you hit isn't here, add it! See the [README](https://github.com/jblaser2/byu-bsg-onboarding).)

## The field & methods

`BSG`
:   **Biophysics Simulation Group** — our group.

`CryoEM`
:   **Cryogenic Electron Microscopy** — imaging flash-frozen biological samples with an electron
    microscope. The broad field. Has two main branches: SPA and CryoET.

`SPA`
:   **Single Particle Analysis** — imaging many copies of a purified molecule and averaging them
    into one high-resolution structure.

`CryoET`
:   **Cryogenic Electron Tomography** — imaging structures *in situ* (inside the cell) as 3D
    tomograms by tilting the sample and combining views. **Our group's focus.**

`Tomogram`
:   The (often blurry) 3D image produced by CryoET.

`STA`
:   **Subtomogram Averaging** — extracting many copies of the same structure from tomograms and
    averaging them to boost resolution. (The flagellar-motor figure on the home page.)

`in situ`
:   Latin for "in position" — imaging a structure *inside* its natural environment (the cell)
    rather than purified in isolation.

`Atomic model`
:   A 3D model specifying where individual atoms sit in a structure — the input to molecular
    dynamics simulations.

`MD simulation`
:   **Molecular Dynamics simulation** — computationally simulating how atoms and molecules move and
    interact over time.

## Structures we study

`T4SS`
:   **Type IV Secretion System** — a molecular machine bacteria use to move molecules across their
    membranes. (The effector-protein-and-plug animation on the home page.)

`Flagellar motor`
:   The rotary molecular motor that spins a bacterium's flagellum, letting it swim.

`Effector protein`
:   A protein that a secretion system delivers to produce an effect (e.g. on a host cell).

## Research & career

`PI`
:   **Principal Investigator** — the professor who heads a research group (for us, Dr. Hart).

`Grant`
:   A funding award from an agency or foundation that pays for the research.

`NSF` / `DOE` / `NIH`
:   **National Science Foundation** / **Department of Energy** / **National Institutes of Health** —
    major U.S. agencies that fund scientific research.

`Publication / paper`
:   A peer-reviewed article reporting research in a scientific journal — how the field shares and
    builds on work.

`Conference`
:   A multi-day meeting where researchers present work, find collaborators, and learn the latest.
    See [Conferences](conferences.md).

## Computing

`Supercomputer / HPC`
:   **High-Performance Computing** — powerful shared machines (accessed remotely, no screen) for big
    storage and heavy computation. See [Getting Started](getting-started.md).

`SSH`
:   **Secure Shell** — the protocol you use to connect to the supercomputer from your own computer.

`GPU`
:   **Graphics Processing Unit** — hardware that accelerates the parallel math behind simulations
    and machine learning.

`Linux / Unix`
:   The operating-system family the supercomputer runs. macOS is Unix-based, so it's similar enough
    to practice on.

`ML`
:   **Machine Learning** — see the [Machine Learning](machine-learning.md) page. (Reminder: it's
    just math.)
